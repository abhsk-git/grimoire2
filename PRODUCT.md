# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Two overlapping audiences:
- **Solo writers and thinkers**: People who write essays, save the links that fed each piece, and want a personal archive their future self will want to inherit. Reflective, long-form, not in a hurry.
- **Developers and technical users**: People who want a personal CMS/blog + bookmark manager with a tool-forward aesthetic. The geek theme caters to this group specifically.

Both users share a common job: turn scattered thinking (links, drafts, ideas) into something durable and publishable.

## Product Purpose

Grimoire is a personal writing tool with a memory. It lets users write and publish posts at their own URL, save the links and notes that informed each piece, and build a compounding archive over time. The explore feed makes the archive public and discoverable by others.

Success looks like: a writer returns after six months and finds their past links, drafts, and published posts exactly where they left them, organized and searchable — and their writing has a public presence that reflects their voice.

## Brand Personality

Quiet confidence, deliberate craft, archival depth.

Three words: **quiet · durable · personal**

Emotional goals: calm focus (not productivity-hype), the satisfaction of a well-tended archive, a slight sense of ceremony around writing.

## Anti-references

- **Medium / bland editorial**: Clean but forgettable, generic serif-on-white, no personality, writing-platform sameness.
- **SaaS landing page patterns**: Eyebrow-on-every-section, hero-metric grids, gradient text, identical card grids, numbered section scaffolding as default.
- **Productivity-bloated tools**: Notion-style visual complexity, nested-docs feel, everything-tool chaos.

## Design Principles

1. **Quiet over loud**: Every element should reduce noise, not add it. The interface recedes so the writing comes forward.
2. **Earn the accent**: Color and emphasis are deliberate, not decorative. The accent signals something real.
3. **Archival weight**: The aesthetic should feel like something worth keeping — not trendy SaaS, not a throwaway app.
4. **Dual-audience without compromise**: The three themes (light/dark/geek) let each audience feel at home without making the default experience feel like it's for someone else.
5. **Show the depth without overwhelming it**: Progressive disclosure — the power is there, but it doesn't shout.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA minimum (4.5:1 body text contrast)
- Keyboard navigation for all core workflows (writing, navigation, search)
- Reduced motion support for animations
- All three themes must maintain readable contrast ratios
